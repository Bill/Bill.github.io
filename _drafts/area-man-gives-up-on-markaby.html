I love Markaby. HTML syntax just sucks and the Ruby DSL is a big improvement. Ruby expressions and blocks take care of generating close tags for you. No more angle brackets.

Things start to break down, however, when Markaby meets Rails. I feel pretty sure that Markaby actually works -- it's just that I can't juggle a sufficiently rich mental model of the whole complex environment:

Markaby -- uses instance_eval in .mab templates (views) so that all methods are evaluated on a Markaby builder object -- putting Markaby in control and simplifying syntax. It even has magic (so magic it hurts)

ActionView
ActionView::Helpers
Named Routes

Others are having similar difficulties:

http://www.timocracy.com/articles/2006/10/11/1-markaby-and-rails-named-routes-and-form_for
http://www.ruby-forum.com/topic/75567#116235

An episode of the Dallas Ruby Brigade:
http://groups-beta.google.com/group/dallasrb/browse_thread/thread/8484702b6a03cb98/0bcd2e35e977da6c?hl=en

When I started using Rails 1.2 (RC) I started seeing exceptions from Markaby views to this effect "you're trying to access a protected member of the view" when calling named routes (e.g. person_url). That was the final straw. Rather than trying to figure out whether it was my problem, Markaby's or Rails' I just decided to give in, stop pushing the rope, and say goodbye to Markaby.

I'll miss your terseness Markaby. But I need all the minutes I can get to iron out my UJS code.
